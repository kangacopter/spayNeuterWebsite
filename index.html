welcome

Having pets is great. Dogs and cats, of course, are the most common pets in our households. What would you do without your dear Fido or Fuzzy? What would they do without you, and without a home? However many pets you have, they are only a small fraction of the large pet population. Unfortunately, many pets do not have homes and find themselves out in the wild or in shelters. Often, animals in the shelter are euthanized because they never find homes. Stray pets are at risk with the elements, vehicles, or other wild animals.

Fortunately, people can take action to improve the huge pet population by becoming responsible pet owners.

As you will find by exploring this website, responsibility includes: spaying or neutering your pet to prevent unwanted litters, and/or considering adopting your new pet instead of supporting breeders. You can also support your local shelter by donating your time, pet supplies, or- of course- money. Please use the menu in the corner to get started!
